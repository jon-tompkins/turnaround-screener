## Thesis in one paragraph

DoubleVerify is the market leader in digital ad verification (fraud detection,
brand safety, viewability), a near-duopoly with Integral Ad Science (IAS) that
benefits from structurally growing digital ad spend and increasing regulatory
requirements around brand safety. The stock has fallen 37% from its 52-week high
after three consecutive EPS misses driven by late-quarter retail campaign
pullbacks and macro-driven ad spend softness. But the underlying business is
still growing revenue 8-10% (management's 2026 guide), gross margins are 82%, and
the CEO + 3 senior officers just purchased ~579K shares in June 2026 — one of the
strongest insider cluster buying signals on the screener. At ~9x EV/EBITDA and
~8.5x forward earnings, the market is pricing permanent stagnation. If DV
returns to beating estimates at Q2 2026 (Aug 5), the re-rating could be sharp.

## Why this could fail

### 1. EPS misses continue — execution problem, not timing
Three consecutive misses (Q3 2025: -17.4%, Q4 2025: -4.9%, Q1 2026: -12.3%)
could indicate a structural inability to forecast quarters, not just timing
noise. If the ad market is softening and DV's forecasting models can't keep
up, another miss at Q2 could break the thesis entirely.

### 2. IAS is gaining competitive ground
Integral Ad Science is the direct competitor and has been aggressive on
pricing. If IAS wins marquee clients or forces DV to discount, gross margins
could compress from 82% — the entire thesis depends on premium pricing power.

### 3. Macro-driven ad spend pullback accelerates
The Q4 2025 miss was attributed to "late-quarter retail campaign pullbacks."
In a broader macro slowdown, ad verification revenue is not immune — it's a
derived demand from digital ad spend. While verification is stickier than
ad placement, a meaningful ad recession would hurt growth.

### 4. AI disruption from Google/Meta native tools
Google and Meta are building native brand safety and viewability tools into
their platforms. If they make third-party verification redundant for their
walled gardens, DV's addressable market shrinks. This is the long-term
existential risk.

### 5. Options are thin — position sizing is constrained
Only 3 expiries, total call OI of 3,663. Building a meaningful options
position is difficult. This favors spot size over leveraged options strategies.
