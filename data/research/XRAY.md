## Thesis in one paragraph

DENTSPLY SIRONA is the largest global dental products manufacturer, trading at

destruction. A new CEO is executing a **$120M annual cost-out restructuring**
while redirecting the eliminated dividend to debt reduction and share
repurchases. The critical signal: **six directors just bought shares at
$9.88–$10.14 in June 2026** — the largest cluster of insider purchases in years.
With Q1 2026 revenue stable, restructuring savings beginning to flow, and a
Return-to-Growth Action Plan targeting sequential improvement by late 2026, this
is a deep-value setup where insiders are voting with their own money that the
bottom is in.

### 2. The valuation is deeply discounted

At $10.92, XRAY trades at:
- **7.0x forward PE** (vs S&P 500 healthcare equipment average ~20x)
- **7.7x EV/EBITDA** (vs peers at 12-18x)
- **1.16x EV/Revenue** (vs peers at 3-5x)
- **0.34x price-to-sales**

The market is pricing this as a permanent value trap — and it may be — but
the margin of safety at these multiples is substantial. Even modest operational
improvement could drive a significant re-rating.

### 3. The restructuring is real and quantified

The $120M annual cost savings program (announced Feb 2026) is significant
relative to:
- Market cap: $2.18B → $120M = 5.5% of market cap annually
- Operating income: ~$125M → could nearly double operating margins
- FCF: $160M in Q4 2025 alone provides the funding

Capital allocation shift:
- Dividend **eliminated** → capital redirected to debt reduction + buybacks
- Net debt/EBITDA at 3.0x → target trajectory to ~2.0x over 2-3 years
- Share repurchases at 0.34x EV/sales are highly accretive

### 4. The business has entrenched competitive moats

Founded in 1877. Largest global dental products manufacturer. The
end-to-end portfolio (imaging, CAD/CAM, implants, orthodontics, consumables)
creates cross-selling opportunities and switching costs that are hard to
replicate. Distribution relationships with dental dealers worldwide are
decades deep. The DS Core cloud platform is a digital moat play that ties
dentists into the ecosystem.

### 5. Sequential improvement is the tell

Q1 2026 revenue was **stable** (not declining further) and in line with
expectations. The company maintained full-year outlook. EMEA and Asia Pacific
are growing. The US business (the problem child) is being restructured.
If sequential revenue improvement arrives by late 2026 as guided, the stock
re-rates quickly from 7x to 12-15x forward — that's 70-110% upside.

## Why this could fail

### 1. A decade of disappointment destroys trust

The 2016 DENTSPLY/Sirona merger has been a value destruction saga. Multiple
turnaround attempts have failed. New CEOs have come and gone. The market may
be right that this is a structurally broken company in a declining dental
market. "This time is different" is the most expensive phrase in investing.

### 2. Cost cuts ≠ growth

The $120M restructuring is all cost-side. Revenue is still declining (0.1%
TTM growth, -4.3% constant currency in FY2025). Without top-line recovery,
the cost savings flow through once and then you're left with a shrinking
business at slightly better margins. The market won't pay 15x EBITDA for a
no-growth dental supplier.

### 3. Execution risk on restructuring

Large restructurings often face:
- Severance and implementation costs eating the savings
- Disruption to sales force and customer relationships during transition
- Key talent departure
- The $120M target may be aspirational

### 4. Dental market secular headwinds

- Aging dentist workforce → fewer procedures per practice
- DDS consolidators (Heartland, Aspen) squeeze supplier margins
- Clear aligner competition (Invisalign, Sparky) commoditizing ortho
- Reimbursement pressure from dental insurance
- Tariff exposure on international supply chain

### 5. Balance sheet risk remains

$2.34B debt, $190M cash, 3.0x net leverage. If EBITDA contracts further
(or doesn't recover), deleveraging stalls and the equity risk premium
expands further. The dividend elimination was necessary but signals distress.
