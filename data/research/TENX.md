---

## The setup in one paragraph

Tenax is a single-asset Phase 3 biotech betting on **oral levosimendan (TNX-103) for
pulmonary hypertension associated with heart failure with preserved ejection fraction
(PH-HFpEF).** The LEVEL Phase 3 closed screening in March 2026 with 230 patients
randomized 1:1. Topline data is expected **Q3 2026** — likely August-September. The
company is capitalized through readout and into Q1 2028 ($118.8M cash, $60M annual
burn). At today's $11.88 with consensus mean target $22.50 (max $30), the market is
pricing roughly 50/50 odds on a positive readout.

This is the highest-conviction binary event in the screener output. It is also a
real Phase 3 — meaning ~50% historical base rate failure, and HFpEF is the
graveyard of cardiology.

---

## Why this is interesting

### 1. Mechanism is real and validated

Levosimendan is approved in 60 countries as **IV simdax** for acute decompensated
heart failure (Orion Pharma's franchise, decades on market). It is a
**dual-mechanism agent** — calcium sensitizer (improves contractility without raising
oxygen demand) AND ATP-dependent potassium channel activator (causes vasodilation,
including pulmonary vasodilation). The Phase 2 HELP trial (published JACC Heart
Failure 2021) showed **+29m improvement in 6MWD vs placebo** (95% CI 3-56m, p=0.033)
with hemodynamic confirmation of reduced PCWP at exercise.

That HELP result is the foundation of the bull case. It's not a single-arm Phase 2
or biomarker study — it's randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, with the
exact same primary endpoint as LEVEL (6MWD change).

### 2. Trial is well-designed and well-powered

LEVEL Phase 3:
- 230 patients (115/arm) with PH-HFpEF
- 1:1 randomization, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- Dose: 2mg/day weeks 1-4, escalating to 3mg/day weeks 5-12
- Primary endpoint: **change from baseline in 6MWD at Week 26**
- Secondary: KCCQ symptom score, NYHA class
- **Blinded sample size reassessment in December 2025 confirmed >90% statistical power** — Tenax did a planned interim sample-size check and the data supported the original 230-patient plan with >90% power to detect the prespecified effect

The BSSR detail is important. Most Phase 3 trials are powered at 80%. Tenax saying
they're at >90% power means either (a) the prespecified effect size estimate (likely
~30-35m, based on HELP) is being met or beaten in the blinded pooled data, or (b)
the population variance is lower than assumed. Either is good news — and the
company would not have communicated the BSSR result if it was bad.

### 3. Capitalized through readout AND through next trial

- $118.8M cash at Q1 2026
- ~$60M annual burn ($15.7M Q1 2026 net loss × 4)
- Runway through Q1 2028 — that's well past LEVEL readout (Q3 2026) and into LEVEL-2 if needed
- **No debt** — clean balance sheet, no covenant risk

This matters because biotech investors get hammered when a Phase 3 succeeds but the
company has to do dilutive financing to fund the NDA filing + commercial launch. At
Tenax's burn rate and cash, they don't need to raise until late 2027 at earliest. If
Phase 3 hits, they raise at $25-30 (post-readout) at much better terms.

### 4. Massive unmet need with no competitor approved

PH-HFpEF is a real disease. HFpEF affects ~3-4M US patients; roughly 30-50% develop
secondary PH. There is **no FDA-approved drug for PH-HFpEF specifically**.
- Sildenafil (Viagra) failed in the RELAX trial
- Riociguat (BAY's Adempas) approved in IPAH but not HFpEF
- Sotatercept (Merck Winrevair) approved for PAH; CADENCE Phase 2 was positive in PH-HFpEF — Merck is moving forward but Phase 3 hasn't started, so Tenax has 2-3 year first-mover advantage if LEVEL succeeds

Pricing/economics: even at modest 5% market penetration of ~1.5M target patients
at $20-25K/year, that's $1.5-2B annual revenue potential. A $20-30 stock would be
appropriate for that NPV.

## Why this could fail

### 1. HELP was IV; LEVEL is oral — PK translation risk

This is the biggest risk by far. HELP demonstrated levosimendan works in PH-HFpEF
**when delivered intravenously for 6 weeks**. LEVEL is testing whether daily oral
dosing (2-3mg/day) achieves sufficient drug exposure to reproduce that effect over
26 weeks.

Levosimendan's PK is complicated. The parent drug has a half-life of ~1 hour, but
its active metabolite OR-1896 has a half-life of ~75-80 hours (~3 days). Steady-
state with daily oral dosing should be reached around day 14-21. So the trial's
4-week ramp + 8-week maintenance should be sufficient time at steady state, but
the assumption that oral exposures translate to the IV HELP effect is unproven.

**This is what would kill the trade.** The drug works IV; the question is whether
oral works as well, and the trial is the test.

### 2. HFpEF is the cardiology graveyard

In the last 20 years of HFpEF Phase 3 trials:
- **PEP-CHF** (perindopril) — failed
- **CHARM-Preserved** (candesartan) — failed primary endpoint
- **I-PRESERVE** (irbesartan) — failed
- **TOPCAT** (spironolactone) — failed (with regional twist)
- **PARAGON-HF** (sacubitril/valsartan) — failed primary endpoint, but FDA approved on subgroup
- **EMPEROR-Preserved** (empagliflozin) — SUCCESS
- **DELIVER** (dapagliflozin) — SUCCESS
- **STEP-HFpEF** (semaglutide) — SUCCESS (obesity subset)

Until SGLT2 inhibitors broke through in 2021-2022, HFpEF Phase 3 success rate was
essentially zero. The SGLT2/GLP-1 wins reset expectations, but for novel
mechanisms (calcium sensitizers) in narrow subsets (PH-HFpEF), base rate of
success is probably **40-50%, not 50-60%.**

### 3. 6MWD endpoint can be noisy in HFpEF populations

6-minute walk distance is a real, FDA-accepted endpoint in PAH (used for Tracleer,
Tyvaso, etc.). But in HFpEF populations the patients are typically more deconditioned
and the test-retest variability is higher. A 29m effect (from HELP) is at the lower
end of what's typically considered clinically meaningful (30-50m is the standard
benchmark). If LEVEL's effect attenuates to 20-25m, the trial might miss
statistical significance even if the drug works.

### 4. Single endpoint = single point of failure

LEVEL's primary endpoint is 6MWD at Week 26. There's no hierarchy of co-primary
endpoints, no fallback. If 6MWD is non-significant, the trial is negative — even
if KCCQ symptoms and NYHA class are positive. That's a regulatory failure even if
the drug clinically helps.

### 5. Competitive overhang from Merck/Winrevair

Sotatercept (Winrevair) had positive Phase 2 CADENCE data in PH-HFpEF. Merck is
likely to pursue Phase 3. If Winrevair launches first in PH-HFpEF (3-4 years out),
Tenax becomes a second-line therapy at best. Sotatercept is a more potent agent
(targets the BMP/activin pathway directly), and Merck has $11B+ commercial
infrastructure. This caps Tenax's eventual market share even if LEVEL succeeds.

- P(success) × $25 + P(failure) × $5 = 0.50 × $25 + 0.50 × $5 = **$15** expected value
- Current price $11.88 → expected upside 26%
- σ ≈ $10 (range $5 → $25)

**Bullish case math:**
- 0.60 × $28 + 0.40 × $5 = $16.80 + $2 = **$18.80 EV**
- 58% expected upside

**Bearish case math:**
- 0.40 × $22 + 0.60 × $5 = $8.80 + $3 = **$11.80 EV**
- Roughly at current price; market is pricing this scenario already

The trade only makes sense if your P(success) ≥ 50%. The BSSR result + HELP data
+ capitalization argue mildly above that, in my view 50-55%.

## Position-sizing + entry/exit plan

### Sizing

This is a binary event with asymmetric payoff (2x up, 0.5x down on event day).
- **Spot position**: 0.5-1.0% of portfolio
  - At a 1% sizing, max loss in failure scenario ~0.6%; max gain in success ~1.5%
  - Acceptable for a high-conviction biotech bet
- **Don't go bigger than 1.5%** — the IV-to-oral PK risk is genuinely unknowable, and a single failed trial in a 17M-share float will gap to whatever the cash floor is

### Entry

- **Buy 1/2 now** at current ~$11.88. Sufficient time pre-readout, valuation reasonable.
- **Add 1/2 on weakness** to $9.50-10.50 zone if it materializes (1-2 standard
  deviation pullback before the readout is normal for biotech pre-event)
- Avoid chasing if the stock runs above $14 pre-readout without news — that's
  retail-positioning territory

### Exit plan

**On success (Q3 2026 readout positive):**
- Stock likely gaps to $20-25 immediately
- **Trim 1/3 in the first day** to lock in the binary outcome
- **Sell another 1/3 at $25-30** as analysts raise targets
- **Keep final 1/3 for the M&A optionality** (single-asset Phase 3 winners often get acquired within 12 months); set a 1-year stop loss at $18

**On failure (Q3 2026 readout negative):**
- Stock likely halves to $5-7 immediately
- **Sell into the morning press release** if possible (the first wave of selling is usually less brutal than the afternoon)
- **Stop at $6** for the position; accept a ~50% loss on the spot piece
- Do NOT average down — the thesis is broken, the company has to decide between LEVEL-2 with a different formulation or running out of cash by 2028

**On indeterminate ("mixed" readout, e.g., trend positive but p>0.05):**
- This is the worst scenario — stock would likely fall 30-40% but still has some option value
- **Sell 1/2 immediately** at whatever opening price you get; the second half is a long-tail bet on LEVEL-2 or regulatory leniency
- Re-evaluate based on company commentary about LEVEL-2 modifications

### Hedges (for larger positions)

For positions >1% of portfolio, consider:
- **Sep or Oct 2026 $10 puts** — likely costs 10-15% of position size; locks in
  worst-case downside at $10 - put cost
- **Pair trade against Merck (MRK)** as a competitive hedge — long TENX, short
  small basket of MRK if Winrevair PH-HFpEF success is the main competitor risk.
  (This is more relevant for a 2027+ view than the 2026 readout)

## Specific watch items between now and readout

1. **Tenax PR cadence** — they have been telegraphing trial progress. Any tightening of "Q3 2026" to a specific month (e.g. "August 2026" or "early Q3") suggests they're close to data lock. Watch for 8-K filings.

2. **LEVEL-2 enrollment updates** — LEVEL-2 is the parallel/confirmatory Phase 3 (
   global). Current enrollment rate signals whether they're betting on a
   single-trial NDA or a two-trial package.

3. **Cash position at Q2 2026 earnings (mid-August)** — should be ~$103M (= $118M -
   $15M Q2 burn). If lower, indicates they're spending faster than expected, which
   either signals trial site expansion or other commercial readiness work.

4. **Any FDA correspondence disclosure** — if Tenax has had recent FDA Type C
   meetings, look for disclosures in the 10-Q about endpoint discussions. A
   late-stage endpoint change would be a yellow flag.

5. **Insider transactions** — currently 0.12% insider ownership is low. Any open-
   market buying by executives between now and readout would be a strong signal
   (they have access to blinded pooled data, even if not unblinded data, and
   buying ahead of a binary event is regulated insider trading territory).
