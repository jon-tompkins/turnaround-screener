stock from $25 to $10. The Q1 2026 earnings beat (EPS $3.16 vs $1.05 estimate, +202%)

At 6.6x forward PE with $2.18B in revenue, the market is pricing near-collapse — any
continued stabilization is asymmetric to the upside.

## Why this is interesting

### 1. Elevidys label update is done — regulatory overhang removed

In November 2025, FDA approved updated prescribing information for ELEVIDYS:
- Boxed warning for acute serious liver injury / acute liver failure
- Removal of non-ambulatory patients from the label
- Enhanced monitoring (weekly for 3 months post-infusion) and modified corticosteroid regimen
- New warning regarding increased susceptibility for serious infections due to immunosuppression

This is significant because the label update process — initiated after a non-ambulatory
patient death from acute liver failure — is now COMPLETE. The worst-case regulatory
scenario (withdrawal) did not happen. Sarepta voluntarily suspended non-ambulatory shipments
in June 2025 and is planning an additional study of enhanced immunosuppression for that
population. The ambulatory patient population remains under treatment, and the boxed warning
is manageable — it's standard for gene therapies.

### 2. Q1 2026 was a massive beat — the franchise is profitable

Q1 2026 (reported May 6):
- EPS: $3.16 vs $1.05 estimate (+202% surprise)
- Revenue: ~$560M quarterly run-rate ($2.18B TTM)
- Operating margins: 49% (though likely inflated by one-time items)
- Free cash flow: $204.8M TTM

The beat shows that even with Elevidys safety concerns, the exon-skipping drugs
(EXONDYS 51, VYONDYS 53, AMONDYS 45) plus Elevidys in ambulatory patients generate
substantial revenue and profit. The forward PE of 6.6x suggests the market expects
earnings to collapse — but if stabilization continues, the stock is dramatically
underpriced.

### 3. siRNA pipeline is the second pillar

Sarepta is quietly building an siRNA (RNA interference) platform:
- **SRP-1001**: Targets DUX4 for facioscapulohumeral muscular dystrophy (FSHD) —
  Phase 1/2a ongoing, early biomarker activity reported March 2026
- **SRP-1003**: Targets DMPK for myotonic dystrophy type 1 (DM1) —
  Phase 1/2a ongoing, early tolerability and biomarker data reported March 2026

Both programs address rare diseases with no approved therapies — large unmet need.
If these read out positively in Phase 2/3, they could become meaningful revenue drivers
by 2028-2029. The market is giving SRPT essentially zero credit for this pipeline.

### 4. Insider accumulation at these levels

Directors have been accumulating:
- Net insider purchases: 172,461 shares over 6 months (13 buy transactions vs 1 sale)
- 4.2% of insider shares bought, only 0.2% sold
- Only sale: Director Stephen Mayo sold 7,239 shares at $17.02 (minimal)
- Multiple directors received stock grants at $0 (standard compensation, but shows
  alignment)

With 28.6% short interest, any positive catalyst (Q2 beat, siRNA data, regulatory
update) could trigger a significant short squeeze.

### 5. Balance sheet adequate for runway

- $653M cash vs $1.05B debt — debt-to-equity 69.6%
- Current ratio 4.6 — comfortable short-term liquidity
- Free cash flow positive at $204.8M TTM
- Revenue $2.18B provides a substantial base

The debt load is real but serviceable given the cash flow generation. If the company
continues to pay down debt from the Wella-style divestiture playbook (though no current
non-core assets to sell), the balance sheet improves further.

## Why this could fail

### 1. Another Elevidys safety event would be catastrophic

The November 2025 label update followed a patient death from acute liver failure. If
another serious adverse event occurs — especially in an ambulatory patient — the FDA
could impose a REMS (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) with severe restrictions,
or in the worst case, suspend the BLA. One more death could destroy the franchise.

### 2. Revenue is still declining

Revenue growth is -1.9% TTM. Elevidys safety concerns reduced patient adoption, and
the non-ambulatory suspension removed a patient population. If Q2 2026 shows accelerating
decline, the stabilization narrative falls apart.

### 3. siRNA pipeline is early-stage

Phase 1/2a data is notoriously unreliable for predicting Phase 3 outcomes. Biomarker
activity in early trials does not guarantee clinical efficacy. The siRNA programs are
years from potential approval and could fail at any stage. Giving them significant
valuation credit is speculative.

### 4. High short interest cuts both ways

While 28.6% short interest can fuel a squeeze, it also means sophisticated investors
are betting aggressively against the stock. The short thesis likely centers on: (a)
Elevidys revenue decline accelerating, (b) another safety event, (c) pipeline failure,
(d) debt servicing concerns. The shorts may have information or analysis that's correct.

### 5. Operating margins are inflated

The 49% operating margin in Q1 2026 is likely boosted by one-time items (milestone
payments, collaboration revenue, or accounting adjustments). Sustainable operating
margins are probably much lower — possibly negative if R&D is fully loaded. The
profit margin of 3% (net) is more representative of the true earnings power.

### 6. Management calling 2026 a "reset year"

When management explicitly calls a year a "reset," it's code for "expect bad numbers
before good numbers." The safety events "reshaped perceptions of gene therapy" per
management. Resetting perceptions takes time — potentially multiple quarters.
