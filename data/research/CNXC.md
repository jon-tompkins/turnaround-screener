# CNXC — Concentrix Corporation

**Research note · 2026-06-15 · post-Webhelp CX services turnaround**

Price $27.12 · Mkt cap $1.65B · EV $7.07B · Cash $223M · Debt $5.69B
Float ~45.5M / 61M total shares · 20% short · 17.5% insider held (incl. GBL)

---

## The setup in one paragraph

Concentrix is a $10B-revenue customer experience (CX) services company trading at
0.71x EV/Revenue and 5.7x EV/EBITDA after a 64% stock decline from $62 highs. The
crash was driven by the 2023 Webhelp acquisition, which added $5.7B in debt and
triggered a $1.5B goodwill impairment in FY2025 (ending Nov 2025). Insiders (CEO,
CFO) are buying in the $27-28 range, but major shareholder Groupe Bruxelles Lambert
 exited 6M shares at $22.25 in April 2026. The June 29 Q2 earnings report is the
immediate binary catalyst — guidance on debt paydown, margin recovery, and AI revenue
 traction could confirm the turnaround or send the stock to new lows.

---

## Why this is interesting

### 1. Deeply cheap on fundamental metrics

At 0.71x EV/Revenue and 5.7x EV/EBITDA, CNXC trades at distressed levels despite
generating $722M operating cash flow and $517M FCF on a TTM basis. Price/book at
0.59x implies the market is pricing in further write-downs or permanent value
destruction. If margins recover to even mid-single-digit operating rates on $10B+
revenue, the equity is worth substantially more.

### 2. AI is both a threat and a tailwind — and management is playing offense

Q1 2026 highlighted "strong AI-driven growth" in banking and e-commerce. Management
is positioning GenAI/agentic AI as a revenue growth catalyst (new product lines,
higher-value services) rather than just a cost-reduction story. If AI adoption
drives net new client wins and pricing power on top of the existing $10B base,
margin expansion becomes credible.

### 3. Insider buying at these levels

CEO Christopher Caldwell bought 1,000 shares at $37.07 (Jan 2026) and 1,000 at
$26.97 (March 2026). CFO Andre Valentine bought 2,500 at $27.95 (April 2026) —
not massive dollar amounts, but directionally positive from both the CEO and CFO
after the stock's decline. They're buying near the lows.

### 4. Leverage reduction story is investable

With $517M FCF and a stated commitment to debt paydown, CNXC could reduce net debt
by $500M+ per year. Over 2-3 years of disciplined delevering, the balance sheet
transforms from distressed to manageable, and the stock re-rates from a levered
distressed multiple to a services company multiple (8-12x EBITDA).

### 5. Basing pattern with defined risk

The stock is 26% below its 200-day MA, 10% below its 100-day MA, and has been
making higher lows since March. The $22 level (prior low, GBL exit price) is
strong support. Downside is defined; upside on a positive earnings surprise is
40-60%.

---

## Why this could fail

### 1. GBL exit is a negative signal

Groupe Bruxelles Lambert sold 6M shares at $22.25 in April — a $133.5M block that
represents a major insider/sponsor exit. While GBL's stake may have been strategic
rather than conviction-driven, the optics are bad and the 6M-share overhang may still
be absorbing demand.

### 2. Debt is existential-risk level

$5.69B debt on $1.65B market cap. D/E of 204x. Net debt/EBITDA is ~10x. This isn't
just high leverage — it's the kind of leverage where two bad quarters can trigger
covenant concerns. Interest costs alone consume a significant chunk of operating
income.

### 3. The goodwill impairment may not be done

FY2025 saw a $1.528B impairment charge (net loss of -$1.279B). If the Webhelp
acquisition continues to underperform or macro deteriorates further, more write-downs
could follow. Each write-down erodes book value and makes the P/B "cheapness"
illusory.

### 4. CX services are structurally threatened by AI

GenAI agents can handle 30-70% of current CX workflows. If enterprise clients
accelerate AI self-service adoption, Concentrix's labor-intensive model faces
secular margin pressure even as it tries to sell AI-as-a-service. The transition
risk is real and poorly understood.

### 5. Short interest at 20% isn't necessarily bullish

20% short is both potential fuel for a squeeze and a sign that smart money sees
real downside risk. Short sellers have been right about CX disruption for 2+ years.
