## The setup in one paragraph

Enphase is the dominant US microinverter company, recovering from a brutal solar reset
that took the stock from $73.74 to $25.78. Three consecutive EPS beats, Q2 2026 guidance
of $280-310M signaling revenue stabilization, and a draft FCC proposal to ban Chinese
inverters from the US grid create a compelling recovery setup. At $49.84 the stock is
26.7% above its 200-day MA with momentum building, but still 32% below the 52-week high.
The combination of regulatory tailwind (FCC ban displacing Chinese competitors), fundamental
stabilization (revenue decline decelerating), and insider confidence (CEO buying at higher
prices) makes this the highest-conviction name in today's screen.

---

## Why this is interesting

### 1. FCC Chinese inverter ban is a direct market-share transfer

The Trump administration is drafting an FCC rule to ban imports of new foreign inverter
models, driven by national security concerns about Chinese-made inverters (Sungrow, Huawei)
potentially disrupting US power supplies. This follows the EU's May 2026 ban on Chinese
inverters in publicly funded energy projects. The FCC has already imposed similar bans on
Chinese drones (December 2025) and routers (March 2026) — none of the waiver applications
from Chinese firms have been granted. If the inverter ban follows the same trajectory,
Enphase is the primary US beneficiary: it's the market leader in residential microinverters,
and installers would be forced to shift to trusted US suppliers. The rule could be published
as early as late 2026.

### 2. Revenue stabilization is real — three beats and guidance holding

Enphase has beaten EPS estimates three quarters in a row:
- Q3 2025: $0.90 vs $0.66 est (+37%)
- Q4 2025: $0.71 vs $0.58 est (+21%)
- Q1 2026: $0.47 vs $0.45 est (+5.5%)

Q2 2026 guidance of $280-310M (consensus ~$291M) signals that the revenue decline (-20.6%
YoY) is decelerating. The solar inventory destocking cycle that crushed 2024-2025 is
running its course. Analysts at Benzinga estimate Q2 EPS of $0.40 on $290.91M revenue —
a bar that's achievable if European weakness stabilizes and US residential solar continues
its modest recovery.

### 3. Insider buying at higher prices

CEO Badri Kothandaraman personally purchased 5,000 shares at $67.37-68.39 in May 2026 —
well above the current $49.84 price. Director Shanker Trivedi bought 1,000 shares at $53.91
in June. Net insider buying over 6 months: 47,949 shares across 16 transactions vs only
4 sale transactions. This is meaningful signal from the person with the most information
about the company's trajectory.

### 4. Balance sheet supports patience

- $931M cash vs $612M debt = net cash position of $319M
- Current ratio 3.8 — no liquidity pressure
- Free cash flow of $91.6M TTM — self-funding
- Gross margins 27.2% — compressed from historical ~40% but stabilizing

### 5. AI data center TAM emerging

Enphase's GaN-based microinverters are being evaluated for small-scale "edge" data centers
and commercial AI hubs. If even a fraction of the AI data center power demand flows to
distributed solar+storage+microinverter solutions, it opens a massive new TAM beyond
residential solar. This is speculative but optionality that the current valuation doesn't
price in.

## Why this could fail

### 1. Q2 2026 earnings miss on July 28

The stock is pricing in stabilization. If Q2 revenue comes in below $280M (low end of
guidance) or Q3 guidance is disappointing, the recovery pattern breaks and the stock
retests the $30s. One bad quarter could undo the entire momentum thesis.

### 2. Solar remains rate-sensitive

Residential solar economics depend on financing costs. If the Fed turns hawkish or mortgage
rates spike, the nascent recovery in solar installations could reverse. Enphase is highly
leveraged to US residential solar volumes.

### 3. FCC ban could be watered down or shelved

The Reuters report explicitly notes the proposal "could still be modified or shelved
altogether." The Trump administration has a track record of shelving China restrictions
during trade negotiations. If the ban is delayed or weakened, the market-share tailwind
narrative collapses.

### 4. Valuation is rich for a declining-revenue company

At 49.7x trailing PE and 5.99x P/B, ENPH is not cheap. The forward PE of 20.8x assumes
earnings recovery that may not materialize on the timeline the market expects. If revenue
growth doesn't return by H2 2026, multiple compression is a real risk.

### 5. Competition from string inverters

SolarEdge and others compete on cost. String inverter + optimizer solutions are
$1,500-2,500 cheaper per 8kW system. In a high-rate environment, cost-conscious installers
may shift away from microinverters. Enphase's 25-year warranty and no-single-point-of-failure
advantages are real but installers are price-sensitive.
