# ACHC — Acadia Healthcare Company, Inc.

**Thesis:** Acadia is the largest standalone behavioral health provider in the US, trading at distressed multiples (P/B 1.18x, EV/Revenue 1.5x) after a 70% stock decline driven by a DOJ investigation, $1B goodwill impairment, and complete C-suite turnover. With returning CEO Debbie Osteen back at the helm, two consecutive blockbuster earnings beats (+39.7% and +593% EPS surprise), and a structural demand tailwind that won't reverse (60%+ utilization increase since 2018), the setup is classic turnaround: hated stock, improving fundamentals, forced sellers exiting. The DOJ overhang is real but increasingly priced in after a $179M securities settlement and a $19.85M Medicare fraud settlement already announced. The risk/reward is asymmetric if Q2 2026 earnings confirm the operational recovery.

---

## Why This Is Interesting

1. **Returning proven CEO.** Debbie Osteen led Acadia from Dec 2018 to Mar 2022 through a period of significant growth. She returned Jan 20, 2026 and immediately signaled operational discipline — $300M capex cut, facility rationalization, and a clear 2026 revenue target of $3.37-3.45B. This isn't a gamble on an outside hire; it's a reunion with a leader who knows the business.

2. **Two consecutive monster EPS beats.** Q4 2025: $0.07 vs $0.01 est (593% surprise). Q1 2026: $0.37 vs $0.26 est (39.7% surprise). The underlying business is performing well even as the stock price implies collapse. Operating cash flow of $182M suggests the headline profit margin (-32.8%) is distorted by one-time items (goodwill impairment, legal charges).

3. **Structural demand tailwind with regulatory moat.** Behavioral health utilization up 60%+ since 2018, psychiatric bed supply shrinking nationally, and Medicare/Medicaid expanding behavioral health coverage. Acadia operates in a segment with severe supply constraints — they can't build beds fast enough, which gives pricing power and fill-rate visibility. This tailwind is policy-resistant (bipartisan support for mental health funding).

4. **Distressed valuation with margin for error.** P/B of 1.18x means you're paying roughly liquidation value. EV/EBITDA of 8.6x is cheap for healthcare services. Even normalizing margins back to 2022 levels (~15% operating margin vs current 10%), the stock is worth $35+ on conservative multiples — ~40% upside from current levels.

5. **Heavy short interest creates mechanical upside.** 38.6% short float means any positive catalyst (earnings beat, DOJ settlement progress, analyst upgrades) forces aggressive covering. The short thesis relies on existential legal risk; any reduction in that risk compresses the short case rapidly.

---

## Why This Could Fail

1. **DOJ criminal investigation is open-ended.** Unlike civil settlements (which have been partially resolved), the criminal probe has no defined timeline or ceiling. An adverse outcome could force asset sales, consent decrees limiting operations, or fines well beyond the $550M in settlements already disclosed. This is the existential risk.

2. **Negative free cash flow.** Despite positive operating cash flow ($182M), FCF is -$217M, indicating significant capital requirements or one-time charges eating cash. If FCF doesn't turn positive by late 2026, the stock reprices lower as dilution or asset sales become necessary.

3. **Executive turnover risk.** The entire C-suite turned over in early 2026. Osteen is back, but the bench is thin — interim CFO (David Duckworth, also a returnee), and new directors still getting oriented. Operational errors during transition could miss a quarter.

4. **Facility rationalization cuts both ways.** Closing underperforming facilities improves margins but reduces revenue scale and could signal deeper problems in the portfolio. If closures accelerate, it raises questions about the core asset quality.

5. **Debt overhang.** $2.7B in debt against $158M cash and $2.3B market cap. Any further credit deterioration or covenant breach could trigger restructuring. Interest coverage requires sustained operating performance — one bad quarter could be terminal for the equity.
