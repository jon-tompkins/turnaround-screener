# CERT — Certara, Inc.

**Research note · 2026-06-17 · Biosimulation pure-play transition**

Price $6.125 · Mkt cap $953M · EV $1.1B · Cash $150M · Debt $305M
Float ~107M / 156M total shares · 2.6% insider · ~113% institutional (over 100% = short)
Short interest 28.6% of float · 10 analysts, consensus Buy, mean target $7.48

---

## The setup in one paragraph

Certara is a biosimulation software company transitioning from a mixed
software-plus-services model to a higher-margin pure-play biosimulation platform.
The stock has been crushed from $14+ to ~$6 on flat revenue guidance (0-4% for 2026),
a Q4 2025 net loss, and Q1 2026 net loss of $8.8M. The bull case: software bookings
grew 20% Y/Y in Q1, the regulatory writing business (dragging margins) was divested
for $85M, EQT Partners owns 26% as a PE anchor, and FDA MIDD tailwinds create
structural demand. The question is whether the "transition year" thesis converts to
margin expansion before shorts squeeze the stock lower.

---

## Why this is interesting

### 1. P/B 0.92x with real revenue and FCF

At $6.12, CERT trades below book value. That's unusual for a SaaS-ish business with
$420M TTM revenue and $117M FCF (an ~8% FCF yield). The market is pricing in
permanent stagnation, but the software segment is growing 20%.

### 2. Divesting the low-margin regulatory writing unit

The $85M divestiture of the regulatory medical writing business cleans up the mix.
Services bookings were down 14% Y/Y, but that's intentional — management is exiting
low-margin work. Pure-play biosimulation should carry higher margins over time.

### 3. FDA MIDD is a structural tailwind

FDA's model-informed drug development guidance pushes biosimulation adoption in
regulatory submissions. EMA and PMDA are following similar paths. Every new drug
application increasingly benefits from Certara's modeling. This isn't cyclical — it's
a regulatory adoption curve.

### 4. EQT Partners anchor provides governance

EQT Partners' 26% stake means there's PE discipline on capital allocation. If
organic growth doesn't accelerate within 12-18 months, EQT has incentive to
explore strategic alternatives (sale, take-private, etc.).

### 5. Software bookings inflecting

20% Y/Y software bookings growth in Q1 2026, against flat total revenue, confirms the
mix shift is happening. If this acceleration continues in Q2, the transition thesis
gets validated.

---

## Why this could fail

### 1. 28.6% short interest is enormous

Nearly a third of the float is short. Shorts are betting that the transition fails
and revenue declines further. High short interest can create volatility in both
directions — but it means the bear case has real conviction and real capital behind
it.

### 2. Revenue could stagnate or decline

0-4% 2026 guidance (ex-divested unit) is hardly inspiring. If software bookings
don't compensate for the services decline, total revenue could go negative. The
market won't pay up for a shrinking business.

### 3. Net losses in Q4 2025 and Q1 2026

The company lost money two quarters running. Operating margin is only 3.8% TTM.
Getting to the 30-32% adjusted EBITDA target requires significant operating leverage
that hasn't been demonstrated yet.

### 4. Options are nearly illiquid

Only 344 call OI and 72 put OI across 4 expiries. The nearest expiry is tomorrow
(June 18). This makes structured options trades impractical — spot only.

### 5. Large pharma spending remains cautious

Certara's core customers (top-20 global pharma) are cyclically cautious on R&D
spending. If this caution extends into 2027, the biosimulation adoption tailwind
doesn't convert to revenue fast enough.
