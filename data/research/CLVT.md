# CLVT — Clarivate Plc

**Research note · 2026-05-28 · longer-horizon value setup with a hard catalyst**

Price $2.59 · Mkt cap $1.66B · Net debt ~$4.3B · EV ~$5.93B · FY26 FCF guide ~$400M
Q1 2026 Adj EBITDA $241M (margin 41.2%) · Q1 2026 net leverage 4.0x

---

## Thesis in one paragraph

Clarivate is an information services company (Web of Science, ProQuest, Derwent
patent intelligence, Cortellis drug pipeline data) trading at **24% FCF yield and
~7x EV/EBITDA** while every other info-services name in the comp set trades
**15-20x EBITDA**. The Value Creation Plan launched in early 2025 is delivering:
Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 190bp YoY to 41.2%, IP segment ACV
returning to flat after years of decline, A&G segment growing with AI products
showing real adoption. The **L&HC divestiture** (announced Feb 25, 2026 with
Morgan Stanley as advisor) is the hard catalyst: it eliminates the need for
future bond refinancing and could trigger a 10-12x EBITDA re-rating on the
remaining business. This is a classic value-with-catalyst setup distorted by
debt overhang and AI sentiment.

---

## Why the discount exists (and why it should compress)

### The debt overhang

CLVT has $4.5B gross debt against $232M cash. Net leverage at Q1 2026 was 4.0x.
For a software/info-services name, that's at the high end of what equity
investors will pay subscription multiples for. Wolters Kluwer, RELX, S&P Global
all sit at 1-2x leverage — they get the 15-20x multiples partly *because* of low
leverage.

But: management has been **methodically deleveraging**.
- $100M of 2026 secured notes fully redeemed
- $300M of 2028 secured notes targeted for repurchase through this year
- 2026 secured notes maturity essentially neutralized
- $1.8B of senior notes mature 2029; plan is to retire all but $500M with FCF
- Net leverage trajectory: 4.0x → ~2.5x over 2-3 years

### The Onex/sponsor overhang

Clarivate went public via Churchill Capital II SPAC in 2019, sponsored by Onex
and Baring Private Equity Asia. Leonard Green added a stake via the 2020 CPA
Global acquisition. Sponsor selling has been a persistent supply overhang. The
PE holders have been distributing/secondary-offering shares for years, and the
absorption has been weak — every sponsor offering has compressed the multiple.

However: with the stock at $2.59 (vs $25+ peak), sponsors are unlikely to dump
at these levels. The marginal seller pressure should diminish, especially if
the company pursues buybacks post-LSHC sale.

### The AI sentiment overhang

Web of Science (citation analytics), Cortellis (drug pipeline data), Derwent
(patent intelligence) — these are exactly the kinds of curated knowledge
databases that *could* be substituted by AI agents querying the open web.

But the moats here are stronger than at Gartner:
- **Academic publishing/citation tracking** has 50+ years of curated data, strong network effects, and is the de facto standard for research evaluation. AI doesn't have access to the underlying citation graph and the IP-protected metadata.
- **Patent intelligence** has regulatory requirements (USPTO/EPO filing data, legal status tracking) that benefit established providers with long-standing data pipelines.
- **Drug pipeline intelligence (Cortellis)** requires curation of clinical trial registries, conference abstracts, regulatory filings — meaningful manual + algorithmic work that's not easy to commoditize.

Clarivate's own pivot is the right one: rather than fight AI, they're embedding
AI INTO their workflows. Clarivate Academic AI claims 30-60% time reduction on
manual tasks — that's a tools-and-data play (Clarivate is the data, AI is the
delivery). That's the right side of the AI trade.

## The Value Creation Plan and Q1 evidence

Launched early 2025. Targets disclosed by management:
- **200bp margin expansion** in 2026 — Q1 delivered 190bp already (39.3% → 41.2% adj EBITDA margin)
- **~10% FCF growth** to ~$400M midpoint — guide reaffirmed at Q1
- **Organic ACV growth** in subscription/recurring revenue while divesting transactional businesses

Q1 2026 segment evidence:
- **A&G**: 15 new Web of Science business wins in China; Spire University major institutional deal; organic ACV +2%
- **IP**: Renewal rates +100bp; organic ACV trends nearly flat (vs negative for years)
- **L&HC**: Organic revenue nearly +1%; top-20 pharma client win

This is what an operational turnaround looks like at quarter 4-5 of execution.
Not exciting growth, but methodical mix-shift away from declining transactional
businesses and toward subscriptions. EBITDA + FCF growth on flat-to-declining
reported revenue is the textbook signature.

## The L&HC catalyst

### What's happening

- **Announced Feb 25, 2026**: Morgan Stanley retained as financial advisor
- **Active discussions with interested parties** (per company language)
- **L&HC = $389.8M 2025 revenue** = 16% of total company revenue
- Includes Cortellis, DRG Fusion, BioWorld

### What it's worth

Conservative valuation framework using comparable info-services/healthcare data SaaS multiples:

| Multiple | L&HC sale value |
|---|---|
| 3.0x revenue (deep value) | $1.17B |
| 4.0x revenue | $1.56B |
| 5.0x revenue (IQVIA/Norstella comp) | $1.95B |
| 6.5x revenue (premium SaaS) | $2.53B |

**Mid-case: ~$1.8B**. Cortellis is the crown jewel of the segment and the
strongest standalone asset (drug pipeline + competitive intelligence platform).

### What it does to the capital structure

Assume $1.8B sale, with Q2 2026 close (estimate):

| | Pre-sale | Post-sale |
|---|---|---|
| Cash | $232M | ~$2.0B |
| Debt | $4.50B | $4.50B |
| Net debt | $4.27B | $2.5B |
| LTM EBITDA | $880M | ~$760M (less L&HC EBITDA) |
| Net leverage | 4.85x | **3.3x → declining to 2.0-2.5x via FCF** |

**This eliminates the need for the 2029 bond refinancing entirely.** The company
could retire the 2028 secured notes in full plus a sizable chunk of 2029s, using
sale proceeds.

### What it does to the multiple

| | Pre-sale | Post-sale |
|---|---|---|
| EBITDA | $880M | $760M |
| Comp multiple | 7x (depressed) | 10-12x (normalized) |
| EV | $6.16B | $7.60-9.12B |
| Less net debt | $4.27B | $2.0-2.5B |
| **Equity value** | $1.89B | **$5.60-6.62B** |
| **Per share (640M shares)** | **$2.95** | **$8.75-10.34** |

So the sale could re-rate the stock to $8-10, vs current $2.59 = **240-300% upside.**

| | | **Total EV** | **$6.01** |

vs current $2.59 = **+132% expected return on 12-month horizon**.

This is meaningfully different from TENX:
- **TENX**: binary in 2-4 months, 50/50 odds, 2x up / 0.5x down
- **CLVT**: continuous over 12-18 months, ~70% positive probability, 50-250% range

The CLVT setup is what value investors call a **"levered fulcrum"** — the equity
is option-like on operational improvement + debt paydown. Small changes in
EBITDA or sale proceeds translate to large changes in equity value because the
debt is fixed.

## Position-sizing + entry/exit plan

### Sizing

This is appropriate as a **core value position**, not a binary bet.
- **2-3% of portfolio** at entry — much larger than TENX because the risk profile is fundamentally different (longer horizon, more dispersed probability)
- Could scale to 4-5% on confirmation that L&HC sale is progressing

### Entry

- **Start 1/2 of intended position now** at ~$2.59
- **Add 1/4 on weakness** to $2.20-2.40 (typical pullback before catalyst)
- **Add final 1/4 on confirmation** — i.e. when L&HC sale is announced at >$1.5B, or on the LOI press release. That's still going to be at $3-4 zone, but you'll be sizing INTO a known-positive catalyst rather than betting on it

### Exit

This is a 12-18 month thesis. Don't sell on small bounces.
- **First trim at $5.00** (1/3 of position) — that's a partial re-rating, locks in some
- **Second trim at $7.00** (1/3 of position) — most of the catalyst has played out
- **Hold final 1/3 to $8-10+** — long-term ride on the de-levered, focused business
- **Year-1 stop loss at $2.00** — if the thesis is broken (L&HC sale fails AND Value Creation Plan stalls), exit gracefully

### Hedges

For positions >3%:
- **Long-dated puts** are expensive on a $2-3 stock; don't bother
- **Pair against a stronger-balance-sheet info services name** (RELX, Wolters Kluwer) to neutralize sector risk
- **Watch the bond market** — CLVT's 2028 secured notes trade publicly; if they widen by >100bps without news, that's a yellow flag on credit perception

## Specific watch items

### Hard catalysts
1. **L&HC sale announcement**. Mid-2026 is plausible given Morgan Stanley engagement Feb 25, 2026. Watch for any 8-K/press release referencing a definitive agreement or LOI.
2. **Q2 2026 earnings July 29**. Particularly watch:
   - L&HC sale process commentary (any specifics on bidders, timeline)

   - Cash position (should be ~$300-350M ex any sale proceeds)
   - Additional 2028 note retirement announcements

### Soft catalysts
3. **Onex/Leonard Green secondary offering filings** — would create overhang, but at $2.59 sponsors are unlikely to file unless forced
4. **AI product traction in A&G segment** — earnings call mentions of Clarivate Academic AI adoption rates
5. **Bond market behavior** — 2028 secured notes trading at par vs discount indicates market view on refinancing risk

- IP segment ACV reverts to negative
- L&HC sale process pauses or PR firm signals difficulty
- New management departures
- Goodwill impairment charges in Q2 (CLVT has carried significant impairment-prone goodwill from acquisitions)
- Forward guide cut on FCF or margin expansion
