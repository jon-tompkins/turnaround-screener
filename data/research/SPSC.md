# SPSC — SPS Commerce, Inc.

**Thesis:** SPS Commerce is a profitable, debt-light SaaS platform trading at distressed multiples (EV/Revenue 2.4x, EV/EBITDA 10.6x) because Amazon policy changes are crushing its revenue recovery business. But the core platform — ex-Amazon — is growing high single digits with 69% gross margins, four consecutive double-digit EPS beats, and an AI product (MAX) in beta with 400 customers. Management is deploying nearly 100% of FCF into buybacks at these depressed levels. If Amazon headwinds trough by mid-to-late 2026 as guided and AI monetizes, the stock re-rates from distressed to growth SaaS multiples, offering 50-80% upside. The risk/reward is asymmetric because the core business is intact even if the Amazon overhang lasts longer than expected.

---

## Why This Is Interesting

1. **The core franchise is healthy and growing.** Ex-Amazon revenue recovery, the platform business is growing high single digits. Fulfillment segment +8%, recurring revenue +7%, ~54,200 customers, ARPU ~$13,550. This is a real SaaS business with sticky recurring revenue, not a broken company. The selloff conflates a discrete headwind (Amazon policy changes) with the whole platform — that's the mispricing.

2. **Four consecutive double-digit EPS beats show operational quality.** Q2 2025 +13.3%, Q3 2025 +13.1%, Q4 2025 +13.1%, Q1 2026 +12.5%. The company has beaten consensus every quarter since the Amazon headwind emerged, demonstrating cost discipline and cross-sell momentum that offset the revenue drag. Management is executing in a tough environment.

3. **AI product (MAX) is a genuine re-rating catalyst.** MAX is embedded in the SPS agentic supply chain network with access to 300,000 trading connections and billions of transactions. 400 beta customers, with documented ROI (Siete Foods case study: 8% revenue protection from stockout prevention). If MAX converts to paid and scales, it adds a high-margin AI revenue stream that justifies a higher multiple. Unlike "AI washing" stories, MAX has network data depth that incumbents can't replicate.

4. **Aggressive buybacks at distressed valuations.** Nearly 100% of FCF ($47M in Q1 alone) deployed to share repurchases. Management is buying back stock at 2.4x EV/Revenue and ~10x EV/EBITDA — well below intrinsic value for a business with these margins and growth. This permanently reduces share count and accelerates EPS growth once the Amazon overhang clears.

5. **Fortress balance sheet provides downside protection.** $154M cash, only $6.6M total debt, debt/equity of 0.69, current ratio of 2.1x. No liquidity risk, no refinancing risk. The stock can stay "cheap" for a long time without the company facing financial distress — unlike leveraged turnarounds, time is on the shareholder's side.

---

## Why This Could Fail

1. **Amazon headwinds could persist beyond mid-2026.** Management says the Amazon revenue recovery business will trough "somewhere in the middle of this year towards the end of this year." If Amazon makes further policy changes or the recovery business structurally declines rather than troughing, the overhang extends and the stock stays depressed. The timeline is guided, not guaranteed.

2. **AI product (MAX) could fail to monetize.** 400 beta customers is promising, but conversion to paid subscriptions at scale is unproven. If MAX remains a free feature or fails to demonstrate enough ROI to justify a premium, the AI re-rating thesis doesn't work and the stock stays at distressed multiples.

3. **Q2 2026 guidance implies continued deceleration.** Revenue guidance midpoint of $195M implies only ~4% growth, well below the ex-Amazon high single-digit rate. If the Amazon drag is larger than expected or core growth slows in H1, even the full-year $796-802M guidance could be at risk. A guidance cut would trigger a sharp sell-off.

4. **Competitive pressure from larger supply chain platforms.** TrueCommerce (PE-backed), Blue Yonder, Infor Nexus, and E2open compete in adjacent spaces. If any of these players aggressively prices or bundles EDI/supply chain solutions, SPSC's differentiated positioning could erode — especially if the Amazon overhang makes SPSC appear vulnerable to customers evaluating long-term platform choices.

5. **Short interest and sentiment.** 9.8% short float with 5.4x short ratio indicates meaningful bearish conviction. While this creates squeeze potential on positive catalysts, it also means the market is pricing in a credible bear case — if the thesis is wrong, the unwind could be ugly as shorts add on weakness.

## Position Sizing

**Recommended allocation: 3-4% of portfolio**

Justification: This is a high-conviction (7/10) turnaround with a fortress balance sheet and clear catalyst path, but the Amazon overhang introduces timing uncertainty. A 3-4% position provides meaningful exposure without over-committing to a situation where the catalyst (Amazon trough + AI monetization) is guided for H2 2026 but not confirmed. Add on weakness toward $50-52 (approaching 52-week low) where the risk/reward becomes more compelling.

## Entry Plan

- **Initial tranche (40% of position):** At current ~$57. This is already 60% off highs and near the 52-week low of $49.
- **Second tranche (30%):** On a flush toward $50-52 — could be triggered by a broad market drawdown, a negative Amazon policy update, or weak Q2 guidance read-through. This level approaches the 52-week low and offers compelling entry.
- **Final tranche (30%):** Post-Q2 earnings (Aug 6) if Amazon headwinds show signs of troughing and core growth accelerates. Even if the stock has moved 10-15% higher on confirmation, the re-rating upside justifies adding.

**Alternative via options:** Jan 2027 $60 calls offer leveraged upside with defined risk. The stock only needs to be above $60 at expiry for the calls to have intrinsic value, and the maximum loss is the premium paid. Given the 200-day MA is at ~$76 (stock at $57 = 40% of 200w MA), a return to the long-term average by Jan 2027 would generate substantial option upside.

---

## Exit Plan

- **Full position exit:** If Q2 2026 earnings (Aug 6) shows core platform growth slowing below mid-single digits AND Amazon headwinds accelerating rather than troughing. This would invalidate the thesis that the core franchise is intact.
- **Partial trim (30%):** At $80 (pre-Amazon-headwind levels), ring the register on a portion to lock in ~40% gain while letting the remainder ride for AI monetization.
- **Full exit:** At $100+ if AI product MAX demonstrates paid conversion at scale (500+ paying customers, documented revenue contribution) — at that point the stock has re-rated to growth SaaS multiples and the easy money has been made.
- **Behavioral triggers:** If management pauses buybacks (signals they don't see value), or if insider sales (not grants) appear at these levels, re-evaluate the thesis. If a major customer loss (outside the Amazon 3P churn) is disclosed, exit immediately.

---

## Hedges

- For larger positions (4%+): Consider buying puts on a broad SaaS ETF (e.g., HACK or IGV) as a sector hedge. SPSC is now idiosyncratic enough that sector beta is secondary, but a broad tech sell-off would still pressure the stock.
- Short AMZN as a cross-hedge is not recommended — Amazon's stock direction has no correlation with their policy decisions affecting SPSC's revenue recovery business.
